About

The game isn't over at retirement.

Second Innings started with a simple observation: old age homes are full of people who spent forty, fifty, sixty years mastering a craft — and almost no one ever asks them to teach it again. Retirement ends a career. It doesn't end a skill.

In cricket, a team doesn't stop playing when the first innings ends — it comes back stronger, adjusts its line-up, and often plays its best cricket the second time around. We borrowed that idea on purpose. The residents on our team sheet aren't case studies or charity cases. They're mentors: people with a batting order full of decades of experience, ready to pass it on to anyone willing to learn.

Every session booked through Second Innings puts a resident back in the game — not as someone being cared for, but as someone teaching, being paid respect and often being paid outright, and staying connected to a world that too often stops calling once someone moves into a home.
