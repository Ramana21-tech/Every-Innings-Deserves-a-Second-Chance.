The Story

Why "Second Innings"

Most platforms for old age homes ask you to donate. Second Innings asks you to enrol.

Inside almost every old age home is someone who spent forty years running a tailoring shop, teaching a classroom, singing at weddings, or perfecting a pickle recipe passed down three generations. Retirement — and often, family circumstance — moved them into care. It didn't erase what they know.

We borrowed the language of cricket on purpose. A batter's first innings ends, but the game isn't over — there's always a second one, and sometimes it's the one people remember. That's the frame we want for ageing: not decline, but a change of ends.

So instead of a donation button, Second Innings gives residents a mentor profile, a skill, and a booking calendar — and gives you a real reason to visit an old age home: to learn something you can't learn anywhere else.
